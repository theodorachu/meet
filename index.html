about meet

We envision a world where meetings aren't just a part of the process, but also inform it. Meetings should have focus, be productive.


meet makes meetings purposeful again by transforming the way people schedule and perceive meetings.


Never again leave a meeting feeling like nothing transpired. With meet, track your meeting data, create talking points, and understand who is relevant to which parts of a meeting.

  • Needfinding

    We interviewed 4 students and 3 industry professionals about the meeting space. We realized that there were lots of issues surrounding scheduling, planning, and following up on meetings.

  • Points of View, How Might We's, Experience Prototypes

    Using the information gleaned from the needfinding stage and some additional interviews we conducted, we brainstormed various Points of View and How Might We statements. We then tested three experience prototypes.

  • Concept Video

    We created a concept video to present the context of our solution and design ideas to an audience outside our immediate team.

  • Low-Fidelity Prototype

    We sketched out various design realizations, build a low-fidelity prototype of the best idea, and performed usability tests with this prototype.

  • Medium-Fidelity Prototype

    With the insights gained from the usability tests, we then went back to the drawing board. We built out a medium-fidelity prototype of our app using an interactive user interface design tool.

  • Heuristic Evaluation

    After creating our medium-fidelity prototype, we had 4-5 people go through our prototype and do a heuristic evaluation to help us pinpoint what the biggest user painpoints currently were.

  • High-fidelity Prototype

    Finally, we redesigned the user flow and created a working, high-fidelity prototype..

  • Poster and Pitch Slide

    For the Project Fair at the end of the class, we created a poster and pitch slide.
